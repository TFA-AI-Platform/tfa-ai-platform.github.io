Alternative Data

Why Alternative Data Matters

In traditional credit systems, individuals without sufficient credit history — such as young professionals, freelancers, or new residents — are often invisible to financial institutions. However, their real-life behavior already shows clear signals of trustworthiness through consistent payments, subscriptions, and digital transactions.

What's Alternative Data

Data outside traditional credit information systems

Utility Bills

Electricity, Water, Gas payment history

Mobile Payments

Postpaid usage, on-time payments

E-wallet Transactions

PayPay, LinePay, Rakuten Pay

E-commerce History

Purchase frequency, category mix

Subscriptions

Netflix, gym memberships, rent

How TFA Utilizes It

Discover how TFA transforms alternative data into actionable insights.

Data Collection

TFA connects with user-consented sources such as e-wallets, mobile carriers, and utility platforms to gather verified transaction histories.

AI-driven Scoring

Using machine learning, TFA transforms unstructured payment data into predictive features for risk assessment.

Every score is accompanied by a clear explanation — showing both the reasoning and actionable advice for improvement.

Our Features

The service we offer is specifically designed to meet your needs.

Alternative Data Scoring Engine

A scoring system based on alternative data sources (telco, e-commerce, e-wallets, bills, user behavior) – expanding credit assessment capabilities for thin-file and no-file users.

AI-driven Feature Engineering

TFA uses machine learning to extract risk-related features from unstructured data (emails, spending behavior, payment logs…) → generating high-predictive risk signals.

Real-time Decisioning

Infrastructure architecture enables data processing and scoring in under 200ms, ensuring real-time responsiveness for BNPL, consumer finance, and telco lending systems.

Explainable AI (XAI)

Each score comes with detailed explanations and recommendations to help users understand the reasoning and improve their financial behavior. TFA complies with transparency and fairness standards (Fair & Transparent AI) required by Japanese financial institutions.

Continuous Learning & Model Governance

Models are continuously retrained based on real-world data, accurately reflecting emerging market behaviors. Governance processes ensure control over bias, drift, and compliance with legal regulations.

API-first Integration & Scalability

TFA is designed with an API-first philosophy, making it easy to integrate with merchants, banks, telcos, and existing financial systems. Cloud-native, scalable infrastructure allows flexible expansion to handle millions of applications per day.
